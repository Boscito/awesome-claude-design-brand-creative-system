# Airbnb-Inspired Hospitality Creative System

Unofficial educational example inspired by publicly observable hospitality marketplace creative patterns.

## 1. Brand Position & Creative Thesis

### Brand Role
Make travel feel personal, warm, local, and human.

### Audience
Travelers seeking belonging, hosts seeking trust, and people comparing options through emotion as much as price.

### Creative Thesis
Show the feeling of being there before explaining the mechanics of booking.

### Brand Tension
Warm human intimacy balanced with marketplace clarity.

## 2. Visual Theme & Atmosphere

### Overall Feel
Warm, lived-in, inviting, editorial, human.

### Energy Level
Calm to moderately lively. The system should feel like discovering a place, not being sold a room.

### Density
Moderate. Let photography breathe, but provide enough detail to support trust.

### Signature Moves
Warm interiors, host and guest moments, rounded UI, map/list patterns, editorial destination modules, soft coral accents.

## 3. Color Palette & Roles

| Token | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| `--color-coral` | `#ff385c` | Primary brand accent and CTA |
| `--color-ink` | `#222222` | Primary text |
| `--color-muted` | `#717171` | Secondary text |
| `--color-cloud` | `#f7f7f7` | Soft background |
| `--color-white` | `#ffffff` | Cards and clean surfaces |

### Color Behavior
Use warm accent color for action and identity. Keep surfaces clean and soft.

### Image + Color Relationship
Photography should provide warmth and place-specific color. UI should not overtake the image.

## 4. Typography Rules

### Type Personality
Human, clear, friendly, practical.

### Recommended Fonts
Use a rounded or humanist sans for interface and a warm editorial display style where appropriate.

### Hierarchy

| Level | Usage | Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| Display | Destination or campaign moments | Warm, confident, spacious |
| H1-H3 | Page sections | Clear and friendly |
| Body | Explanatory copy | Conversational and useful |
| Caption | Location, host, rating, amenities | Compact and scannable |

### Type Over Images
Use type over images only with clear contrast and enough calm space. Prefer captions beneath images for marketplace clarity.

## 5. Layout Principles

### Composition
Use grids, cards, map/list pairings, and editorial destination layouts.

### Spacing
Comfortable, approachable, never cramped.

### Cropping & Framing
Show enough environment to make the place understandable. Avoid crops that remove the context of the stay.

### Responsive Behavior
On mobile, image-first cards should retain rating, location, and key value cues.

## 6. Component Styling

### Buttons
Rounded, friendly, clear. Coral for primary actions.

### Cards
Image-led with soft radius. Metadata should be easy to scan.

### Navigation
Simple, search-forward, trust-oriented.

### Forms & Inputs
Large touch targets, clear labels, friendly error states.

### Data / Proof Modules
Ratings, reviews, host credibility, amenities, cancellation, safety, and map context.

## 7. Photography & Imagery System

### Imagery Role
Imagery builds trust, desire, and context. It should answer, "What will it feel like to stay here?"

### Subject Matter
Homes, rooms, details, neighborhoods, hosts, guests, food, light through windows, local context, small human rituals.

### Photography Style
Natural lifestyle and interior editorial. Real but polished.

### Composition Rules
Prefer natural perspective, breathable rooms, inviting details, and a clear sense of place.

### Lighting
Natural daylight, warm interior lamps, morning/evening softness. Avoid harsh commercial flash.

### Color Treatment
Warm-neutral, realistic, inviting. Preserve natural materials and skin tones.

### Product Imagery
The "product" is the stay. Show beds, kitchens, views, workspaces, bathrooms, entry moments, and neighborhood proximity.

### People Imagery
Use candid-feeling moments: arriving, hosting, cooking, reading, greeting, relaxing. Avoid forced stock smiles.

### Illustration / Iconography / Abstract Visuals
Use simple icons for amenities and trust signals. Avoid complex illustration unless it supports education.

### Image Quality Bar
An image should make the viewer understand both the space and the feeling of being in it.

## 8. Social Creative System

### Paid Social
Lead with place desire: view, room, neighborhood, or experience. Pair with simple benefit or offer.

### Organic Social
Destination stories, host spotlights, unusual stays, local guides, seasonal travel.

### Carousels
Slide 1: strongest stay or destination image. Slides 2-4: details, amenities, neighborhood, review. Final slide: booking CTA.

### Stories / Reels / Shorts Thumbnails
Use vertical walkthroughs, room reveals, view reveals, and local moments.

### Platform Behavior
Pinterest and Instagram can be image-led and aspirational. Facebook can lean trust and family planning. TikTok can lean walkthrough and discovery.

## 9. Website Creative System

### Hero Sections
Use search and destination imagery together. The first screen should make booking feel easy and emotionally rewarding.

### Product Sections
Show listings, amenities, reviews, map proximity, and host detail.

### Editorial Sections
Use travel guides, local stories, host profiles, and seasonal collections.

### Conversion Sections
Prioritize trust, availability, price clarity, and low-friction next steps.

### Galleries
Use a mix of wide room shots, detail shots, views, and exterior context.

## 10. Motion & Interaction

### Motion Mood
Soft, helpful, subtle.

### Interaction Rules
Hover and transitions should feel calm and responsive.

### Media Motion
Use walkthroughs, subtle map transitions, gallery reveals, and gentle carousel movement.

## 11. Voice In Visual Context

### Copy + Visual Relationship
Copy should clarify the emotional promise and practical details.

### Headline Style
Warm, simple, place-aware.

### Caption Style
Specific: place, host, detail, review, amenity.

## 12. Do / Don't Rules

### Do

- Show real-feeling spaces with context.
- Use photography to communicate warmth and trust.
- Keep marketplace details visible and scannable.

### Don't

- Use sterile hotel imagery.
- Overcrop rooms so the stay becomes unclear.
- Let decorative UI compete with place photography.

## 13. Quality Bar & Review Checklist

The asset should make the viewer feel oriented, welcomed, and confident. If the viewer cannot tell what the space or experience is, the image has failed.

## 14. Agent Prompt Guide

```text
Create hospitality creative from this system. Prioritize warm real-place photography, clear marketplace details, soft UI, and a feeling of belonging. Avoid sterile travel stock imagery.
```

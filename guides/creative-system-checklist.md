# Creative System Checklist

Use this checklist before handing a brand creative `DESIGN.md` to an agent, or after reviewing the agent's first output.

## 1. Strategic Clarity

- The audience is specific enough to shape visual decisions.
- The creative thesis is a usable decision rule, not a slogan.
- The brand tension is clear, such as "technical but warm" or "premium but accessible."
- The document says what the brand should avoid.

## 2. Visual System

- Colors have roles, not just hex values.
- Typography rules explain hierarchy, casing, scale, and restraint.
- Layout rules explain density, spacing, composition, and responsive behavior.
- Component guidance covers buttons, cards, navigation, forms, and proof modules.

## 3. Photography & Imagery

- The document explains what imagery does for the brand.
- Subject matter is specific: people, products, environments, textures, UI, interiors, etc.
- Composition rules include cropping, negative space, scale, and framing.
- Lighting direction is concrete enough to influence image choice.
- Color treatment explains how photos should interact with the palette.
- Product imagery rules explain how to show use, scale, detail, packaging, or outcomes.
- People imagery rules explain expression, casting, styling, and authenticity.
- The doc says when to use photography, screenshots, illustration, abstract visuals, or no image.

## 4. Social Creative

- Paid social rules explain thumb-stopping visual hierarchy.
- Organic social rules explain what feels native to the brand.
- Carousel rules include cover, pacing, and final CTA behavior.
- Vertical format rules include safe zones and crop priorities.
- Platform differences are noted when they matter.

## 5. Website Creative

- Hero guidance explains media, headline placement, CTA behavior, and trust signals.
- Product sections explain how to show benefits and proof.
- Editorial sections explain how to use quotes, stories, and supporting imagery.
- Conversion sections explain how to handle offers, FAQs, pricing, and testimonials.
- Galleries and image grids have rhythm and caption rules.

## 6. Agent Readiness

- Every section gives the agent a choice framework.
- The strongest rules are written as concrete do/don't statements.
- The doc includes prompts for social assets and website assets.
- The quality bar catches generic AI-looking output.
- The doc is specific without becoming so rigid that every output looks identical.
